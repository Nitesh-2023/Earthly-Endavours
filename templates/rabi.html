  • Wheat

  • Grown in both Kharif (June to September) and Rabi (October to March) seasons. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils with a pH around 7. Wheat typically takes about 100 to 150 days to mature, depending on the variety and environmental conditions.

  • Barley

  • Usually sown from April to June, barley thrives in well-drained, fertile soils with a pH of 6.8 to 7.5. The crop requires about 90 to 120 days from planting to harvest, making it a relatively quick-growing option.

  • Peas

  • Best planted in early spring or late summer, peas prefer loamy, well-drained soils with a pH of 6.0 to 7.5. They take approximately 60 to 70 days to mature, allowing for multiple harvests in a growing season.

  • Grams

  • Typically grown in winter (October to November), they thrive in sandy loam to clay loam soils with a pH of 5.5 to 7.0 and require about 140 to 165 days for cultivation, making them suitable for cooler climates.

  • Mustard

  • Usually sown in the Rabi season (September to November), mustard prefers well-drained, loamy soils with a pH of 6.0 to 7.5 and takes about 90 to 120 days to mature.

  • Cotton

  • Planted during the Kharif season (April to July) or Rabi season (September to October), cotton grows best in black cotton soil with a pH of 5.5 to 8.5 and requires about 160 to 180 days for full growth.
