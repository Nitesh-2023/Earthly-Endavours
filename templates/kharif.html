  • Rice

  • Primarily grown in the Kharif season (June to September), rice prefers clayey or loamy soils with good water retention and a pH of around 6.0 to 7.0, taking approximately 120 to 150 days from sowing to harvest.

  • Maize

  • Sown from March to June, maize thrives in well-drained, fertile soils with a pH of 5.8 to 7.0 and typically takes about 90 to 120 days for cultivation.

  • Millet

  • Grown mainly in the Kharif season (June to September), millets prefer well-drained sandy or loamy soils and require around 70 to 90 days for maturity, making them drought-resistant crops ideal for arid regions.

  • Ragi

  • Typically sown during the Kharif season (June), ragi grows best in dry, well-drained soils with a pH of around 6.0 and takes about 90 to 150 days for cultivation.

  • Pulse

  • Generally grown in the Rabi season (October), pulses prefer well-drained soils with a pH of around 6.0 and take approximately 90 to 120 days from sowing.

  • Ground Nuts

  • Best planted in the Kharif season (May), groundnuts thrive in sandy loam soils with good drainage and require about 120 to 150 days for full growth.
