  • Cucumber

  • Grown during the warm months (April to July), cucumbers prefer sandy loam soils rich in organic matter and take about 50 to 70 days from planting.

  • Pumpkin

  • Typically sown from April to June, pumpkins thrive in rich, well-drained soils and take around 75 to 100 days for cultivation.

  • Bitter Guard

  • Best grown in warm climates from April to July, it prefers sandy loam soil with good drainage and takes about 60 to 70 days for maturity.

  • Tomato

  • Grown year-round but primarily during warmer months (March to June), tomatoes prefer well-drained sandy or loamy soils and require about 60 to 85 days from sowing until harvest.
